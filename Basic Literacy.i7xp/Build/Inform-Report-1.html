Problem. The sentence 'The Poet's Notebook is erasable scenery on the desk'   appears to say two things are the same - I am reading 'Poet's Notebook' and 'erasable scenery on the desk' as two different things, and therefore it makes no sense to say that one is the other: it would be like saying that 'the chalk is the cheese'. It would be all right if the second thing were the name of a kind, perhaps with properties: for instance 'Dairy Products School is a lighted room' says that something called Dairy Products School exists and that it is a 'room', which is a kind I know about, combined with a property called 'lighted' which I also know about.

Problem. It would be all right if the second thing were the name of a kind, perhaps with properties: for instance 'Dairy Products School is a lighted room' says that something called Dairy Products School exists and that it is a 'room', which is a kind I know about, combined with a property called 'lighted' which I also know about.

Problem. You wrote 'An erasing tool called an eraser is on the desk'  : but I can only make 'a something called whatever' when the something is a kind I know, possibly qualified with adjectives. For instance, 'an open door called the Marble Door' is fine because 'door' is the name of a kind and 'open' is an adjective which means something for doors. But 'a grand archway called the Great Gates' would not normally mean anything to me, because 'archway' is not one of the standard kinds in Inform. (Try consulting the Kinds index.)
